Welcome to Creative Coding With Elizabeth!

Creative coding, also known as generative art or algorithmic art, is the use of computer programming to make art. I've created a collection of sketches, each of which produces a simple figure as a starting point for creation. Your task is to adjust the parameters to create something visually appealing. The finished piece will combine my programming, your choice of parameter settings, and in some cases pseudorandom aspects generated by the computer. Most activities are initiated using buttons located in the bottom right corner of the window. Click on at any time to see this message again.

Click on to choose a sketch to experiment with. Click the cog icon to adjust the sketch's input parameters. Some sketches also have a build icon , which creates a new picture using a different set of random numbers. When you've created a picture you like then you can think about animating your production if you wish. Click on either Set Start Frame or Set End Frame to make the current picture become either the starting point or the finishing point of the animation. After you have one frame set up then change the parameters again and design another picture to appear at the other end of the animation. Once you have your two frames marked then you're ready to play back your animation using the play icon . You can change how long your animation lasts for by clicking on the arrow next to the play icon. With a subsequent click on the … button you can access some more advanced animation options.

If you want to save your animation as a video file then click on . Click on to save the current image as a photo. You can even add an artist's signature to your creations by clicking in the lower left corner.

The site has been primarily tested in Google Chrome. If you find any broken features (in any browser) then you can report them on GitHub.
