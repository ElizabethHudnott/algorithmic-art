Truchet Tiles show how the simplest of rules can create intricate patterns when applied over and over. Only a small number of design motifs are used to create the picture. The screen is divided into a grid of cells. Each cell contains a simple shape that is randomly selected from a library of designs which you edit from within the program. Some cells may be left entirely blank. The resulting pattern looks like a maze of twisted pipework reminiscent of the 1989 game Pipe Mania.

This sketch was inspired by Daniel Shiffman's Coding Challenge #76 (which he developed from the book 10 PRINT by Montfort et al.) and by Tariq Rashid's Algorithmic Art YouTube channel. The sketch was supported by Talking Birds and you can read an essay chronicling the sketch's development on their blog.

The specific rules activated enforced depend on the kind of tile but may include, for example, ensuring that a t-junction or cross shaped tile connects to at least one horizontally adjacent tile and another tile above or below itself.
Applies changes to the directionality and angles of the chevrons or parallelograms on a per row basis. Use + to make them point one way or - to point the other way or 0 for rectangular tiles. Enter as many symbols as you like, one after another. You can also use +1, for example, to have a row with ¹⁄₁₀th of the effect's intensity.
