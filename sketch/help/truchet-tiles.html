Truchet Tiles show how the simplest of rules can create intricate patterns when applied over and over. Only a small number of design motifs are used to create the picture. The screen is divided into a grid of cells. Each cell contains a simple shape that is randomly selected from a library of designs that you can edit from within the program. Some cells may be left entirely blank. The resulting pattern looks like a maze of twisted pipework reminiscent of the 1989 game Pipe Mania.

This sketch was inspired by Daniel Shiffman's Coding Challenge #76 (which he developed from the book 10 PRINT by Montfort et al.) and by Tariq Rashid's Algorithmic Art YouTube channel. This sketch was supported by Talking Birds.

The specific rules activated by ticking this box depend on the kind of tile but may include, for example, ensuring that a t-junction or cross shaped tile connects to at least one horizontally adjacent tile and another tile above or below itself.
